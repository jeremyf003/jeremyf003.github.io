Technical Projects

E-Ball Model

University of British Columbia | Dec 2025 – Jan 2026

Designed and programmed a Bluetooth-controlled robotic vehicle using three-phase BLDC motors. Developed firmware in C++ on ESP32 using Arduino IDE, integrating ESC motor drivers and implementing PWM-based PWM speed control with DualShock 4 Bluetooth input.

Coin Picking Robot

University of British Columbia | Apr 2025 – May 2025

Developed a robot capable of detecting and picking Canadian coins using embedded systems. Programmed in C on EFM8LB1 and PIC microcontrollers using CrossIDE, and designed an H-bridge motor driver with RF communication between subsystems. Built collaboratively in a team of five.

Reflow Oven Controller

University of British Columbia | Mar 2025 – May 2025

Designed a temperature-controlled reflow soldering oven for PCB assembly. Programmed in A51 on the N76E003 microcontroller and validated thermal performance through testing in a six-person engineering team.

Bluetooth Controlled Car

Personal Project | Aug 2025

Built a Bluetooth-controlled vehicle with full directional movement using an ESP32. Integrated DualShock 4 input and controlled four DC motors via dual L298N motor drivers.

Line Following Robot

Kwantlen Polytechnic University | Apr 2024 – May 2024

Programmed an autonomous robot to detect and follow lines using photosensors. Developed in C on a PIC18F46K42 microcontroller and refined through iterative testing in a team of three.
